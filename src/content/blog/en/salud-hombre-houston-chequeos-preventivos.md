# Men's Health in Houston: Preventive Checkups That Can Save Your Life

Many Hispanic men in Houston only see a doctor when the pain no longer lets them work. The problem is that the most dangerous diseases — high blood pressure, diabetes, prostate problems — advance silently for years without a single symptom. By the time warning signs appear, the damage is often already done.

The good news: a simple preventive checkup, taking less than an hour, can catch these problems early, while they can still be treated.

## Why Should Men Get Regular Checkups?

The statistics are clear: men visit the doctor far less than women, and Hispanic men even less, due to language barriers, work schedules and lack of health insurance. The result is that treatable conditions get diagnosed late.

A regular checkup allows you to:

- Detect high blood pressure before it damages your heart or kidneys
- Identify prediabetes and reverse it with lifestyle changes
- Monitor your prostate starting at age 40-50 with a simple blood test (PSA)
- Know your cholesterol and testosterone levels
- Find infections that cause no symptoms

## Conditions We Treat Frequently

### High Blood Pressure

Hypertension is the "silent killer": it doesn't hurt, gives no warning, and over the years damages the heart, brain and kidneys. One in three adult men has it and many don't know it.

### Type 2 Diabetes

The Hispanic community has a higher risk of developing type 2 diabetes. Catching it at the prediabetes stage makes it possible to reverse it with diet and exercise, without lifelong medication.

### Urinary and Prostate Infections

Burning when urinating, frequent urges to go, or difficulty emptying your bladder are not just "part of getting older": they can be a [urinary tract infection](/services/infecciones-urinarias) or a prostate problem that has a treatment.

### Sexually Transmitted Infections

Many STIs cause no symptoms in men, but they still spread and cause harm. We offer confidential [sexually transmitted disease testing](/services/enfermedades-transmision-sexual), with same-day treatment when possible.

## Warning Signs: Don't Ignore Them

Come to the clinic as soon as possible if you experience:

- Chest pain or pressure, or shortness of breath with exertion
- Difficulty urinating, weak stream or blood in the urine
- Excessive thirst, very frequent urination or unexplained weight loss
- Frequent headaches with blurry vision (possible high blood pressure)
- Constant fatigue that doesn't improve with rest
- Changes in sexual desire or intimate performance
- Lumps, pain or changes in the testicles

None of these symptoms goes away on its own. The sooner you get evaluated, the simpler the treatment usually is.

## Practical Tips to Take Care of Your Health

1. **Get an annual checkup** even if you feel fine — that is exactly the point of prevention.
2. **Know your numbers:** blood pressure, glucose, cholesterol and, after 40, your PSA.
3. **Move 30 minutes a day:** walking counts; you don't need a gym.
4. **Cut back on sodas and fried food** — they are the main drivers of type 2 diabetes.
5. **Don't smoke**, and if you drink alcohol, do it in moderation.
6. **Sleep 7-8 hours:** poor sleep affects testosterone, weight and blood pressure.
7. **Talk about your health:** ask about your family's medical history; many risks are hereditary.
